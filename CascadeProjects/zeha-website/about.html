About ZEHA

Empowering communities through sustainable development since 2009

Our Story

Zambesia Education and Health Aid (ZEHA) was founded in 2009 with a simple yet powerful vision: to create lasting positive change in the communities of Zambesia through comprehensive development programs.

What started as a small grassroots initiative by a group of passionate educators and healthcare workers has grown into a respected non-governmental organization that has touched the lives of over 5,000 individuals across 50+ communities.

Our journey began when our founders witnessed firsthand the challenges facing rural communities in Zambesia - limited access to quality education, inadequate healthcare facilities, persistent poverty, and the growing impacts of climate change. They recognized that sustainable development required a holistic approach that addressed these interconnected challenges simultaneously.

Today, ZEHA stands as a beacon of hope, implementing evidence-based programs that not only address immediate needs but also build long-term capacity within communities to drive their own development.

Our Approach

Community-Centered Development

We believe that sustainable development must be driven by the communities themselves. Our approach involves:

  • Community Consultation: We work closely with local leaders and community members to identify priorities and design programs that meet their specific needs.
  • Capacity Building: We invest in training local individuals to become leaders and implementers of development initiatives.
  • Participatory Implementation: Communities are active participants in all stages of program implementation, ensuring ownership and sustainability.
  • Monitoring & Evaluation: We continuously assess our impact with community feedback to improve and adapt our programs.
